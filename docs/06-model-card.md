# Model Card — Credit Risk Scorecard (PD / LGD / EAD → Expected Loss)

*SR 11-7-style documentation for the v3 model. The companion topic docs are listed in
[`README.md`](README.md); the v3 roadmap is maintained internally.*

> **Why a model card, and why SR 11-7?** SR 11-7 is the U.S. supervisory guidance on *model risk
> management* — the expectation that a model used for real decisions is documented, validated, and
> governed, with its intended use, data, metrics, limitations, and fair-lending posture stated plainly
> so a reviewer (or a future maintainer) can judge whether it's fit for purpose. A credit model that
> sets reserves and prices loans is squarely in scope. This card is that record: it makes the model's
> assumptions and known weaknesses explicit rather than leaving them implicit in the code, which is
> the difference between a model that can be *defended* and one that merely *runs*.

---

## 1. Model overview & intended use

The Credit Risk Scorecard estimates the **Expected Loss** of a consumer instalment loan from
borrower and loan characteristics, decomposed along the regulatory credit-loss identity:

```
Expected Loss (EL) = PD × LGD × EAD
```

and turns it into lender decisions — a discounted **lifetime ECL**, **expected profit / RAROC**, and
**risk-based pricing** (break-even and target-return APR).

**Intended use.** Decision *support* for a human underwriter or portfolio analyst: relative risk
ranking, provisioning, and pricing on Prosper-style unsecured consumer loans.

**Out of scope / not intended for.** This is **not** an automated approve/deny system, and it is
**not** wired to generate ECOA adverse-action letters — per-borrower SHAP drivers are explanatory,
not a compliant reason-code set (see §6, §7). It should not be used on populations materially
different from the training data (see §7 selection bias).

## 2. Model details

| Component | Model | Benchmark |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| **PD** (probability of default) | Calibrated **XGBoost** (isotonic recalibration) | AutoGluon AutoML baseline; Prosper's own grade ranking |
| **LGD** (loss given default, fraction) | **AutoGluon** regressor | Mean-LGD baseline |
| **EAD** (exposure at default, $) | **AutoGluon** regressor | Full-exposure baseline |
| **Default timing** | Discrete-time **hazard** (loan-month panel + XGBoost) → monthly `h(t\|x)` | scikit-survival `RandomSurvivalForest` |

The discrete-time hazard model reshapes the book into one row per loan per month alive (target =
`defaulted_this_month`, with the month index as a feature) so the predicted probability *is* the
monthly hazard. It feeds the **PD term structure** used by the lifetime ECL in
`modeling/common/finance.py`. PD drivers are surfaced per borrower with **SHAP**. Code lives in
`modeling/survival/` (panel build, hazard fit, term structure, scikit-survival benchmark).

## 3. Data

- **Source.** Prosper Loan dataset (~113k loans, 81 columns).
- **Modeling population (PD/LGD/EAD labels).** **Post-2009, terminal** loans only — `Completed` → 0,
  `Defaulted`/`Chargedoff` → 1. Unresolved loans (`Current`, `PastDue*`, `FinalPaymentInProgress`)
  are dropped from the cross-sectional labels because an incomplete performance window would mislabel
  immature vintages as safe. The hazard panel **recovers the `Current` loans** as **censored**
  observations (exposure up to the snapshot), restoring that information honestly.
- **Macro overlay.** National unemployment + fed funds joined **point-in-time at origination**, kept
  in **through-the-cycle (TTC)-anchored** form after the out-of-time study showed raw macro is partly
  a vintage proxy ([`01-feature-engineering.md`](01-feature-engineering.md)).
- **Train/test split.** **Out-of-time (vintage)** at `data.OOT_CUTOFF = 2013-01-01` — train on
  originations before the cutoff, test on/after — the way credit models are actually validated.
- **Feature governance.** Only fields knowable at the underwriting decision are inputs. Outcome
  fields (`LP_*`, `ClosedDate`, delinquency cycles), underwriting-set price fields (`BorrowerRate`,
  `BorrowerAPR`, …), and Prosper's own scores/ratings are excluded as features (the latter kept as
  benchmarks). `DATA_DICTIONARY.md` is the per-variable authority; `data/features.py` is the
  executable manifest.

## 4. Metrics

*(Exact values regenerated by the scripts and stored under `modeling/model-results/`.)*

- **PD discrimination.** Test **AUC 0.7612**. Across XGBoost / LightGBM / Random Forest and a FLAML
  search, discrimination converged at **~0.745–0.750** — the ceiling is **information-bound**, not
  model-bound, on public Prosper data.
- **PD calibration.** Weighted mean |predicted − actual| ≈ **0.017** across deciles
  (`calibration_decile.csv`); by-vintage in `calibration_vintage.csv` (see
  [`03-validation-methodology.md`](03-validation-methodology.md)).
- **Hazard survival metrics** (`hazard_survival_metrics.csv`). Discrete-time hazard **IPCW
  concordance 0.6637** vs scikit-survival RandomSurvivalForest **0.6996** (RSF time-dependent AUC
  **0.7069**, integrated Brier **0.00275**). The production hazard trades a little discrimination for
  monthly-granularity ECL and reuse of the calibration + SHAP stack — a deliberate, documented choice.
- **ECL backtest** (`ecl_backtest_by_vintage.csv`). Overall **predicted/realized dollar ratio 1.02**
  over the resolved book (n = 26,136; 6,350 defaults; ≈ **$31.7M** predicted vs ≈ **$31.2M**
  realized) — the financial engine is calibrated in **dollars**, not just in probability.

## 5. Limitations & assumptions

- **Accuracy ceiling.** ~0.75 AUC on public data; this is an honest ceiling, not a tuning failure.
- **Thin out-of-time vintages.** The post-2013 test vintages are small (2013 n ≈ 2.6k; 2014 n ≈ 69,
  with zero realized loss), so the out-of-time **dollar** read is **directional**, not load-bearing;
  the in-sample 2009–2012 vintages carry the calibration evidence.
- **Undiscounted backtest.** The ECL backtest compares **undiscounted** predicted EL against nominal
  realized `LP_NetPrincipalLoss`; the hazard term structure governs loss *timing* (and the
  discounted ECL used for pricing), but the undiscounted lifetime total is timing-independent.
- **No adverse-action reason codes.** ECOA-compliant reason codes are **not** shipped (deferred).

## 6. Fair-lending treatment of geography & occupation

Macro and per-state features **drive** the model but are **dropped from the per-borrower SHAP reason
codes** (`predictor.explain_pd()`, Phase 0): they are identical across all applicants on a given day,
so they are not valid *personal* adverse-action reasons. `BorrowerState` and `Occupation` are handled
per `DATA_DICTIONARY.md`; Prosper's own scores/ratings are treated as **benchmarks, not features**, to
avoid laundering the incumbent model's policy into ours. Full ECOA adverse-action reason codes are a
documented future item.

## 7. Reject inference / selection bias

Prosper publishes only **funded** loans. The model is therefore **conditional on Prosper's own
approval policy** and does not directly generalize to applicants Prosper rejected. Estimated PD/EL on
a broader applicant pool would be biased by this selection. A future correction would reweight or
apply a Heckman-style selection model using the (separately available) listing/application data; for
now the limitation is acknowledged rather than corrected.
